HTML, the backbone of the World Wide Web, is known for its stability and reliability. Through a workshop at D*sign Week Linz, we explore its accessibility and ease of use by using the most accessible means – pen and paper – to create a collaborative website. By promoting the idea of creating a website without coding knowledge, we are encouraging a broader audience to participate in building a digital archive of the things people want to preserve for the future. This website is built with the inputs gathered in this workshop.

A common misconception is that something can be preserved digitally forever. Even though efforts can be made to extend the lifespan of digitally stored materials, achieving absolute permanence is difficult. Digital content is inherently impermanent. In recognizing this reality, we seek simultaneously digital and analog solutions for the preservation of our archive.
